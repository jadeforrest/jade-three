# Artist Q&A

## Background

I played violin quite a bit in elementary and middle school, taught myself guitar in college, and a little piano. But I wasn't very deep in music — my understanding of pop music was pretty shallow for a lot of my childhood. In high school I most loved reggae and some African music, along with classics like Led Zeppelin and whatever pop I happened to have picked up. I got into music a lot more heavily in college, and was a poser DJ in my 20s — poser because I never really learned to mix records all that well. But alternative music and electronic music totally cracked my shell and opened me to a new world. Making music myself wasn't something I really did much until late 2024.

## Why did you start making music?

I wanted to make more and consume less. I was sick of social media and wanted to do more with my life that felt like an act of creation. Part of it was realising I was avoiding doing things unless I was going to be amazing at them. I thought — I can just do this for fun, and if that's all it is, that's fine. I'd like people to hear it, but honestly I'm doing it for myself. It can be play.

## What does the name Jade Three mean?

My last name is Rubick, which is a cube — three dimensions. It's a reference to my first and last name. I regret it a little because you have to specify whether or not to spell out "Three." But I like that it's a reference to my name.

## Sound & influences

Even though I love a lot of kinds of music, I find a lot of my music tends to sound like soundscapes and soundtracks with an electric vibe. I'm not sure why that is, and whether I'll continue with that or expand. Electronic, alternative, and pop have had the most influence on the way I make music today.

## Process & approach

Ideally, I start with a feeling and try to grab onto it. I find that if I have an emotion going full blast, I can't help but make better music. Early on, I would just find sounds I liked and then start trying to make them sound good together. More recently, I try to create the arc of the story — often starting with an idea that sounds good in 8 bars, and then creating a journey out of that. I'm still early enough in this that the way I go about it is changing almost every time.

## The world as backdrop

Whatever your politics, the world is seeming a lot scarier and uncertain than it did when I grew up. There is a lot to be anxious about, and I expect it to show up in my work unless things get better — and why would I expect that to happen? That said, I'm naturally a pretty positive person. I think it's a way of finding joy within the small things — loving the micro amidst a crazy macro.

## Who do you make music for?

I don't have a lot of expectation of a lot of people listening to my music. It's possible that only ten people will listen to something I write. The person can be different depending on what I'm doing — often it's myself or a friend, but sometimes I'm writing it for the world in a diffuse way. Ideally, I'd love for the music to make them feel something powerfully, or notice something they haven't heard before, or just feel a bit carried with it.

## Goals & direction

I'm learning at a crazy rate, and I hope my music keeps getting better. At some point it would be nice if more people listened to it — but I've got a lot of work to do for that. Some of that is building skills, and some of that is just the network you're in. Billie Eilish was a sudden success, but she also had a social network I'll never have. So I'll be content with the intrinsic joy I get from it!
