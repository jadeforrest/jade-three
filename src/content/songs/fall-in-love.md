# Song Q&A

## What's the song about?

It's about falling for someone even though you know it's probably not a good idea — that feeling of trying to resist the pull. The main lyric is "I don't want to fall in love."

## Where did the idea come from?

The song wasn't originally about that topic at all — the lyrics came after the fact, from vocal clips I found that I liked the sound of. Once the vocals came in, they completely reoriented the song. It sounded a lot different before that.

## How did it come together in the studio/production?

It started with some Splice clips that had a fun, bouncy beat to them. I ended up using those sounds in the more excited parts, but I cut a lot of it back and went with a more sparse sound for most of the song. It ended up more pop-like than anything I've produced before — which I think is actually a good thing. Well-produced pop is often very clean, and this pushed me toward more subtraction and simplicity. I want to do more of that.

## Favourite moment in the track

There are a few. At the 18-second mark when the bass drops, there's a little gap in the sound that makes it really impactful. At 1:13 I love when the bouncy sound comes in the background. But my favourite is around 2:20 when the bridge goes crazy with the string sounds — it builds and builds and then gets a bit nuts. Really fun.

## One thing listeners might miss

The ending is a lot more melancholy than the rest of the song. It felt like it was previewing the direction the relationship went afterwards — like it's telling a continuing story. That was my hope anyway.

## Any lyrics you're especially proud of?

I didn't write the lyrics — I found and used vocal clips. But I liked how it all ended up fitting together.
